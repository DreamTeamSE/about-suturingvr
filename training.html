VR Training Checklist

Before meeting with the patient

  1. Try to arrive ~15 minutes before the patient is scheduled to come in (e.g. if they are scheduled for 9am, come in at 8:45am)
  2. Check Slack in case there are any updates from the coordinators
  3. Sign in at Carey’s office, ask about patient status
  4. Grab all essential equipment and prepare for patient
    1. Turn on the headsets
    2. Check device battery level >75%, charge otherwise
    3. Connect both devices to same wifi
    4. Check that casting is working
      1. Launch SuturingVR
      2. Calibrate the headset
    5. Raise the headset volume to >75%
  5. Sanitize the headset and controllers with non-alcohol sanitizing wipe
    • Don't wipe the lenses of the headset
  6. Wait for "OK" from Carey to see the patient


Informed Consent/Pre-Procedure Survey

  1. Introduce yourself, describe the study, ask if they’ve used VR before, explain VR
  2. Ask the patient if they would like to try VR
  3. If no
    1. Ask if they’d like to be in the control group (no distraction, just surveys)
  4. If yes
    1. Ask about disqualification criteria
    2. If yes, explain side effects of VR
    3. Tell the patient that the VR can be stopped and taken off at any time
    4. Go through controls on controller and website, ask which controller to use
    5. Prepare the headset with SuturingVR (or YouTube) for the patient, let them try while they are sitting
    6. If comfortable, let them try while they are in the position required for procedure
    7. If comfortable, ask if they would be comfortable using for 15-30 minutes during procedure
  5. Go through the informed consent, obtain signatures
  6. Go through pre-procedure survey verbally

During the Procedure

  1. If patient is in control group, don’t need to be in the room with the patient
  2. Be in the room with the patient using VR
  3. Once doctor is ready, set up headset as before while patient is in position for procedure, ensure they are comfortable
  4. Calibrate and start the game
  5. Keep an eye on the patient and game progress
    • Can reset game by holding X/A on other controller, reset calibration by holding trigger

After the Procedure

  1. Take off the headset and turn off
  2. Obtain local anesthetic dose numbers from doctor
  3. Go through post-procedure survey with patient, verbally
  4. Thank the patient for their participation
  5. Obtain vitals from doctor/nurses 30 minutes after, or ask Carey to look up on Epic
  6. Digitize pre- and post-procedure surveys
  7. Sanitize the headset and controllers
  8. Turn off all devices, leave charging in Carey’s office
