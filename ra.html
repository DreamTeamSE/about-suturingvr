Research Assistant Instructions


Collection Code Scheme

Study Coordinators will notify you of collection codes for that day, general scheme is as follows.

Oncology Ward: OC001, OC002, OC003 …………… OC023…………. OC142


Items Needed

- Quest Headset - Turn off completely and put on charger when done with data collection for the day

- Two Quest Controllers

- Carry Case

- Headphones (if desired)

- Tablet/Computer - If Android tablet, install Oculus app via Google Play Store. Otherwise get Google Chrome on it.


Process to use Quest VR

- Exclude individuals with motion sickness from the study

- Try to get an approximate fit of the headset for the patient during the “triage” or informed consent sign up period. This will make it easier to fit the headset directly before the procedure

- Bring the top strap farther down the head, towards ear level. To test if its fitting correctly, have the patient look down (to see if it falls) and see if they can read the text on the menus

- Bring down the back strap as far down as possible (around the curve of the head)

- Signs of a good fit include:

  • The headset fitting snug, even when looking straight down
  • Being able to read the text on the menus without having to tilt or turn their head

- Process for adjusting headset is different for Quest and Quest 2, but we’ll only be using the Quest 2

- To put the glasses spacer in (for patients who wear glasses), remove the black foam part and place the plastic glasses spacer in (round piece inward)

  • Turn on the headset (not on the patient)
    • If not, use the credentials below:
      • Username: eacnresearch@gmail.com
      • Password: EACNresearch1
  • Put on the headset (as the research assistant)
    • Click the triggers on the controllers a few times until they register as pointers on the screen
    • There will black-and-white cameras on the outside of the headset so you can see the room around you
  • Set the “Guardian” which is the play-area boundary that helps establish where it is “safe” to play (making sure you don’t hit walls,etc.).
    • To set the floor level, bring one of the controllers near the floor then press “Confirm” on the screen
    • Set the “Guardian” zone by clicking the associated button on-screen, then by holding the trigger and creating a circle around the room where the headset will be used. Include where the research assistant is and where the patient will be
    • Make it bigger than just the hospital bed (basically the whole room).
      • If this is not done, obnoxious red bars will be visible across the screen until you get back into the “safe” zone
    • Do not use a stationary boundary
  • Enable casting
    • Cast to Mobile Android Device
    • Open the oculus app
      • Log in to the same test user as on the headset, if not done already
    • Press headset/wifi button in the top right
    • Click on the Oculus Quest 2
      • Make sure both the Android device and Quest headset are on the same wifi
    • Click Start
      • As long as the headset is not asleep, you will see the screen as the user is
      • If it is asleep, it will be a black screen
    • Cast to a computer or web browser (NOT Android Tablet)
      • Requires a good internet connection
      • Log into Facebook using test user above
      • Visit https://www.oculus.com/casting on Google Chrome (on a tablet, computer, etc)
      • On the headset, click the curved arrow button on the bottom taskbar
        • If the taskbar is not visible, press the “Oculus Button” on the right controller
      • Click “Cast”
      • Click “Computer”, then “Next”
  • Turn on the game before putting on the patient.
    • Click the 3x3 grid icon on the bottom of the screen, called “Apps”
    • Hover over the “App Lab” box that should say SuturingVR and click on it
    • It will go through a few loading screens, then stop when it says “Press the trigger on your controller”
  • Put the headset on the patient
    • Approximately adjust straps for the patient, put the headset on the patient, then “fine-tune” the adjustments
      • Glasses should be worn while using the headset
    • Text should be clear, not blurry, so that the experience is not disorienting
      • Calibrate the headset?
    • Put the headphones on, if desired
    • If the patient is not facing the “Forest” tree button on starting scene, press and hold the “Oculus” button on the right controller. This will re-center the camera to the correct position
    • Monitor patient and game progress on Oculus app to ensure everything goes smoothly
      • If the game goes off center for some reason, reset orientation with button on Oculus app
    • Reset the game using the controller not used by the patient (left controller if the patient is using the right controller) by pressing the button on the bottom (X or A) and holding for 5 seconds
    • If you need to reset the calibration (before the game), hold the Oculus button
    • To reset the patient’s view (while in the game), hold the trigger on your controller for 5 seconds

Note: We should get an Android Tablet to download the Oculus app. This will allow us to adjust orientation of the screen, help us see what the patient is seeing, and set boundaries.

Note: Place bright stickers for the on/off button and volume buttons


Interaction Examples

Plugging in the Headset to Charge

Hold to power off, press once to set headset to sleep

Hold to re-center view, press once to bring up Oculus menu

Hold on the non-active controller to reset SuturingVR

Push forward to throw fruit in-game, push harder to throw fruit harder


Sanitization

  1. Items Included
    1. Oculus Quest Headset
    2. Oculus Quest Controller
    3. Carrying Case
  2. Process of Cleaning VR
    1. Before procedure:
      1. Wipe down VR and controller with Lysol wipe and microfiber cloth
      2. Help patient wear disposable VR liner
    2. After procedure:
      1. Remove and dispose of disposable VR liner
      2. Wipe down VR and controller with Lysol wipe and microfiber cloth
    3. End of Day:
      1. Wipe down VR and controller with Lysol wipe and microfiber cloth
      2. Wipe down VR carrying case with Lysol wipe
